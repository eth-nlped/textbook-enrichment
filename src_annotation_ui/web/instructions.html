Textbook Enrichment Evaluation
(Annotation Guidelines)
The goal of your task is to help us evaluate our research on Artificial Intelligence (AI) based methods for creating more engaging and effective textbooks for students. You will be evaluating the performance of various AI-based methods that use images obtained from the internet to assign images to sections of a childrenâ€™s textbook.
In the first stage of your evaluation, you will be provided with text from a section of the textbook that has been divided into multiple subsections. Each subsection will be enclosed within a bounding box and will include images at the end. Your task is to evaluate the relevance, usefulness, and type of each image with respect to the text only within the same subsection. You will be asked to answer the following questions regarding these evaluations:
  • Is this image relevant to educational concepts described in this subsection text?
  • Is this image redundant compared to previous images in this subsection?
  • Is this image didactically useful for explaining this subsection text?
  • What is the type of this image?
In the second stage of the evaluation, you will be reviewing the same subsections and images as before. Your task is to evaluate the relevance and redundancy of each image in relation to the text and images within all the subsections in this section. You will be provided with following specific questions to guide your evaluations:
  • Is this image relevant to educational concepts described in this section?
  • Is this image redundant compared to previous images in this section?
Note: It is important to complete the questions for the first stage before moving on to the second stage. Additionally, please make sure to read the sections that do not have any images during the first stage, as they will be useful for answering the questions in the second stage.
Explanation of Response Choices for Questions

(will also be shown during the annotation)

Choices concerning relevancy questions:
  • relevant: some concept described in the subsection/section text is picturised in the image;
  • somewhat-relevant: image is loosely on the same subject/topic domain as the subsection/section text, but is not exactly about the same concepts;
  • irrelevant: not at all related to the subsection/section text.
  • undecided: unable to make a decision, maybe because of my low understanding of the text or image.

Choices concerning redundancy questions:
  • yes: this image illustrates exactly the same concept as one of the previously shown images in the section;
  • no: this image illustrates atleast some unique concepts as compared to the previously shown images in the section;
  • undecided: unable to make a decision, maybe because I do not understand the concepts illustrated by this image.

Choices concerning didactic-usefulness questions:
  • yes: image will be appropriate for teaching the subsection/section to a student - I would like it here (with an appropriate caption) if I was learning from this textbook;
  • no: image is not appropriate for teaching the subsection/section text;
  • undecided: unable to make the decision, maybe because of my less understanding of the text or image.
